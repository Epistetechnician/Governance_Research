      1. Hold AAVE

      Since AIP 1, AAVE became the new governance token of the Aave Ecosystem. AAVE and/or stkAAVE (AAVE staked in the Safety Module) token holders receive governance powers proportionally to the sum of their balance. There are initially two powers associated with each governance token:

      • The proposal power that gives access to creating and sustaining a proposal.
      • The voting power which is used to vote for or against existing proposals.
      1. Create a proposal

      All proposals must have their own PR on Aave AIP Github repo and follow the template. Proposals can first be discussed on the governance forum where informal signaling polls can be created.

      There are two kinds of proposals:

      • Standard process proposals that aim at changing the Aave protocol and the Ecosystem reserve. They will be executed by the short-time-locked executor.
      • Slow process proposals that aim at changing the governance itself, and its tokens. They will be executed by the long-time-locked executor.

      The creator of the proposal needs to have a higher proposal power than the threshold and maintain this condition until the proposal is actually executed. Proposition power threshold is 0.5% for standard process proposals and 2% for slow process proposals.

      As an initial safeguard mechanism, a guardian account i.e., a temporary 5/10 multisig is given a power of veto to cancel at any time, any proposition. The current multisig signers can be found here.

      1. Vote

      There is no minimum voting power required to vote. Voting only occurs on the app governance portal and a vote lasts 3 days for standard process proposals and 10 days for slow process proposals. Users can use both AAVE or stkAAVE to vote. Votes can be changed following the common voting process. Previous votes will automatically be canceled.

      For a vote to be valid, the amount in the wallet used to vote must remain equal or higher than the tokens used to vote throughout the entire proposal voting and validating period.

      1. Results of the vote

      2 factors must be known regarding the results of a vote:

      • Quorum is 2% for standard process proposals and 20% for slow process proposals.
      • The difference between for-votes and against-votes (in % of total voting power) needs to exceed the vote power differential which is 0.5% for standard process proposals and 15% days for slow process proposals.
      1. Implementation

      Short-time-locked and long-time-locked executors are in charge of implementing proposals. A successful proposal is queued and has to be executed after the execution delay (1 day for standard process proposals and 7 days for slow process proposals) and before expiration (5 days for all proposals).

      1. Delegation

      AAVE holders have the choice between delegating proposal creation power to specialized actors while holding their voting rights; or even delegating them to another entity. They can delegate their powers here. There is no delegation transitivity i.e. a user that has received delegated power cannot forward this delegated power to another delegatee.

Maker Governance Process

1. Participating in Maker Governance:

MIP0 (Maker Improvement Proposal 0) introduced the Maker improvement proposals (MIPs framework into the Maker ecosystem. A Maker Improvement Proposal (MIP) is a document that regulates and defines the behavior of Maker Governance, MakerDAO, or the Maker Protocol. They are standardized documents voted upon by Maker Governance that can be added, amended, replaced, and removed. This first MIP provides the necessary templates, processes, and guidelines for working within the framework and defines the key roles required for operating the MIPs Process.

1a. Holding MKR Token

The MKR token is the governance token for the Maker Protocol that allows MIPs to be voted on effectively, as well as a recapitalization source when the Maker Protocol is running at a deficit. That means holders of the token can vote on changes to the Maker Protocol and have strong incentives to govern the system well. Anyone can submit proposals for an MKR vote, not just MKR holders. The main responsibility for MKR holders is to use voting power to participate in Governance Polls and Executive Votes.

  • Governance Polls are on-chain proposals that measure the sentiment of the MKR voter’s ecosystem
  • Executive Votes are on-chain proposals that execute technical changes to the Maker Protocol through continuous approval voting where the state of the system is continuously represented encourage continuous governance

    (Every Executive Vote is preceded by a Governance Vote)

1b. Creating Proposals

Proposals are introduced to the community, discussed, and put up for a Governance Poll and Executive Vote as necessary. If you want to propose something to the Maker community, you need to create a PR (pull request) on the Maker MIP Github repo. Your proposal must follow the templates and it's a good idea to first discuss it on the governance forum to get feedback and see if there is enough support. If there is, you can have a formal vote on the governance portal.

Proposals may be divided into two categories:

  • Proactive proposals suggest changes to the system and provide a timeframe for executing those changes.
  • Reactive proposals are made in response to changes in the state of the system.

There are two proposal types in each category:

  • General Proposals should be used for proposals whenever a more specific ratified template is not more appropriate
  • Technical Proposals should be used when proposing changes to the smart contract code within the Maker Protocol

To ensure further clarity and completeness, proposals are classified through general and process components. Majority of the time, process MIPs introduce sub proposals for different components which give context to general MIPs components. After following the templates to post in the Maker forum and then creating a pull request in MIPs Github repository the proposal will be likely be approved by a MIP editor. If the editor’s verification of a proposal is successful then it receives is form MIP number based on it’s components and is merged in the pull request (which is just adding a new MIP into the main codebase).

The proposal is ready for Request for Comments, or RFC, is a period of time in which the community is invited to review a draft and offer feedback. This period lasts for at least three months, and is followed by a "frozen period" of at least one month during which no further changes are made. After this phase a proposal is ready for formal submission according to the governance cycle, usually during the first 3 days of the first week of every month.

Whether you’re a creating an opportunity for a governance poll where you propose to vote in a new team or for a executive vote to introduce a new collateral type, the process is robust enough giving the opportunity to anyone to enact meaningful change to the system.

1c. Voting Participation

Voting is conducted primarily on the governance portal. To use must lock up your MKR in the voting contract because only locked MKR token. When voting with MKR you need to use your wallet to decide whether to vote by locking your maker in a executive voting contract or instead voting in the governance polling contract.
