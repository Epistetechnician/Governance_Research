Maker Governance Process

Participating in Maker Governance:

Maker Improvement Proposal 0 introduced the Maker improvement proposals (MIPs) framework into the Maker ecosystem which provided the foundational steps towards participatory design in MakerDAO’s governance process. This first MIP provides the necessary templates, processes, and guidelines for working with the framework and defines the key roles required for operating the MIPs Process. Creating a robust governance infrastructure through the mechanisms built within a participatory design system helps strengthen the Maker Protocol and Dai. The steps for participation in MakerDAO governance are as follows:

1. Holding MKR Token

The MKR token is the governance token for the Maker Protocol that allows MIPs to be voted on effectively, as well as a recapitalization source when the Maker Protocol is running at a deficit. That means holders of the token can vote on changes to the Maker Protocol and have strong incentives to govern the system well especially in a risk setting like Dai defaulting. Anyone can submit proposals for an MKR vote, not just MKR holders. The main responsibility of MKR holders is to use voting power to participate in Governance Polls and Executive Votes.

  • Governance Polls are on-chain proposals that measure the sentiment of the MKR voter’s in the Maker ecosystem
  • Executive Votes are on-chain proposals that execute technical changes to the Maker Protocol through continuous approval voting where the state of the system is continuously represented to encourage continuous governance

    (Every Executive Vote is usually preceded by a Governance Vote)

2. Creating On-Chain Proposals

The MakerDAO governance process has three specific proposal formats for members to participate in governance: Signal Requests, Declarations of Intent, and Maker Improvement Proposals (MIPs). Proposals are introduced to the community, discussed, and put up for a Governance Poll and Executive Vote as necessary.

Proposals may be divided into two categories:

  • Proactive proposals suggest changes to the system and provide a timeframe for executing those changes.
  • Reactive proposals are made in response to changes in the state of the system.

2.1 Signal Requests

These are short proposals used to make changes to the existing parameters in the MakerDAO smart contract system. Successful Signal Requests are aggregated and voted on in weekly executive votes. Using the Signal Request Checklist authors can assure community members’ receptiveness to vote accurately on the forum poll. Based on the results of the off-chain poll an author will either edit their requested signal thread or notify the governance facilitators to push it on-chain. Signal requests are a necessary proposal format, especially for off-chain governance.

  • Recent Signal Request:

    Signal Request MIP65

2.2 Declarations of Intent

MIP13 introduced declarations of intent, which allow Maker Governance to signal an intention to the wider community. A declaration of intent is a statement that declares what Maker Governance plans to do. This is so that people will know what Maker Governance is up to and can help them plan their actions. In many cases, a declaration of intent can be off-chain as a signal request but depending on the specificity and brevity of the declaration it can be on-chain as multiple MIPs.

  • Recent Declaration of Intent:

    Declaration of Intent MIP13c-SP12

2.3 Maker Improvement Proposals (MIPs)

MIPs are standardized documents that become subject to a vote eventually to further regulate as well as define the behaviors of the Maker network between the protocol and governance. Think of them as mini constitutions where MIPs can be added as amendments or replaced and removed. MIPs can be separated into sets and types to have interdependent MIPs that change with one another generally or technically. MIPs are the most time-intensive proposal format due to the monthly governance cycle and time it takes to recieve feedback on the governance forum. MIPs define as well as address a specific behavior in the Maker network.

There are two proposal types:

  • General Proposals should be used for proposals whenever a more specific ratified template is not more appropriate
  • Technical Proposals should be used when proposing changes to the smart contract code within the Maker Protocol

Proposal Ideas:

MIPs usually start from an author who has an idea for a MIP and creates a proposal on the MakerDAO forum in the governance proposal ideas category. This is where a proposal can be reviewed and edited to eventually create a PR (pull request) on the Maker MIP Github repo. The proposal must generally follow these templates and it's a good idea to first discuss the proposal idea on the governance forum to get feedback and see if there is enough support. If there is, you can have a formal vote on the governance portal.

To ensure further clarity and completeness, proposals are classified through general and process components. The majority of the time, process MIPs introduce sub-proposals for different components which give context to general MIPs components. After following the templates to post in the Maker forum and then creating a pull request in the MIPs Github repository the proposal will likely be approved by a MIP editor. If the editor’s verification of a proposal is successful then it receives its formal MIP number based on its set of components and is merged with the pull request to be accepted as a Request for Comments (which is just adding a new MIP into the main codebase).

The Requests for Comments (RFC):

The Request for Comments is a period of time in which the community is invited to review a draft and offer feedback after a MIP editor verifies the proposal. This period lasts for at least three months and is followed by a "frozen period" of at least one month during which no further changes are made to the proposal. Following this period, the submitted proposal will be ready for formal submission.

Formal Submissions (FS):

After this phase, a proposal is ready for formal submission according to the governance cycle, usually during the first 3 days of the first week of every month. Whether you’re creating an opportunity for a governance poll where you propose to vote in a new team or for an executive vote to introduce a new collateral type, the process is simple enough to allow anyone permission to enact meaningful change to the system.

  • Recent MIP:

    MIP84

3. Voting Participation On-Chain

Voting is conducted primarily on the governance portal. When voting with MKR you need to use your wallet to decide whether to vote by locking your maker in an executive voting contract or instead voting in the governance polling contract. For results of a vote, majorities always rule because no quorums are required to pass a vote unless it is an MIP which in that case requires a quorum of 10 thousand MKR. Furthermore, you can instead choose to delegate your voting power using the delegates contract to a recognized delegator. This is aimed to incentivize all MKR token holders to participate in proposals by making sure votes are passed to prevent malicious or just bad proposals from passing a vote with low voter participation. This requires the DAO’s governing body to be always engaged to make sure votes are representative of the decentralization that exists in the current state of the system.

3.2 Governance Polls

Governance Polls are on-chain and occur to measure the sentiment of MKR voters. They use a ranked-choice voting mechanism where voters can select multiple options with the ranked choices being prioritized for the next poll. Proposals are put to a ‘yes’ or ‘no’ vote within a determined set time period usually at most a week. These are generally one-off proposals that make the polls specifically used for:

  • Determining the DAO and governance processes in the applied non-technical layer of Maker’s Protocol
  • Forming consensus on community values through specific strategic goals and the respective targets
  • Measuring people's opinions on possible Executive Vote proposals
  • Agreeing on governance proposals that come from the MakerDAO forum signal threads
  • Deciding what values certain system parameters should be set to before those values are confirmed in an executive vote
  • Agreeing on risk parameters for new collateral types as presented by Risk Teams

3.3 Executive Voting

Executive votes are on-chain and occur to execute technical changes to the Maker Protocol. Using continuous approval voting, when an executive vote is active it has proposed changes that are being made on the protocol’s smart contracts in parallel. Proposals are put to a ‘yes’ or ‘no’ vote within maybe a set time period. Executive votes are likely to be used for:

  • Adding or removing collateral types and vault types
  • Adjusting global system and vault-specific parameters
  • Replacing modular smart-contracts

4. MakerDAO Governance Contributor Lifecycle

The lifecycle of a MakerDAO governance contributor starts with simply holding the MKR token and evolves into an almost perpetually multi-faceted role. The unifying mechanism for the various contributors entering the MakerDAO governance pipeline is voting. Voting serves as a great onboarding technique for new members and is a low-effort, high-impact form of participation in terms of time needed and relative importance. By having this at the beginning of the contributor lifecycle, contributors become more knowledgeable about the structural technical and non-technical processes taking place in and outside of the DAO. Contributors are likely to realize soon into their life as a contributor that MakerDAO exists to maintain Dai as a collateralized stablecoin through collateralized debt positions. Allowing new members to develop trust for the Maker ecosystem through voting to learn to earn establishes an effective way for governance contributors to realize the importance of protecting the integrity and stability of the governance infrastructure itself. This is where a contributor’s conviction will likely lead them to participate in risk management through proactive and reactive governance functions. Meaning they choose to compete and cooperate structurally to implement or intervene in situations requiring decentralized risk management.

Once realizing the responsibility for governing the system by establishing the status quo while managing risk collectively. The cycle is a recursive feedback loop where governance contributors are stakeholders that are accountable for themselves and other stakeholders so the DAO and the assets it protects are controlled most effectively. Even though governing MakerDAO through iterative change will always require constant involvement. This responsibility is the encouragement to find new ways to contribute to the integrity of the Maker ecosystem and the overall supply of Dai. Being part of a growing ecosystem causes your path as a contributor to change, but participating in a governance process like MakerDAOs shows the importance of simple features to help maintain the system and of continuous input from the community and body of governance. The lifecycle of a MakerDAO governance contributor may be impermanent, but through a modular approach to change Maker benefits greatly from a contributor’s mobility.

Gnosis Improvement Proposal #64

Snapshot Proposal:

Summary:

As decided in GIP-29, GnosisDAO will receive 15% of the total SAFE token supply over 4 years. They suggest using the SAFE tokens to support the main objective of GnosisDAO, which is to make the Gnosis Chain successful. GnosisDAO becomes more successful by distributing SAFE tokens because it decentralizes the Gnosis Chain network as a whole. A 94.22% majority of voting members are for the distribution of SAFE tokens to incentivize decentralizing Gnosis Chain through GnosisDAO. The value proposition of this proposal is that it is a foundational step in positioning Gnosis Chain as the most decentralized network available. To support this objective, they suggest distributing the SAFE tokens as additional yield to those participating in staking on the Gnosis Beacon Chain. The SAFE tokens should be distributed equally among GNO holders every month until the end of the locking period (February 15, 2023). After the end of the locking period, up to 9.1% of the remaining SAFE tokens should be used as additional yield for stakers on the Gnosis Beacon Chain over the remaining vesting period. The latter distribution should be based on the importance of the different staking categories, such as individual stakers, distributed liquid staking, professional staking services, and liquid staking.

What the overall distribution will look like:

Until February 15th all GNO staked will be treated equally in terms of distribution. After then the different types of stakers will receive different percentages of the SAFE token rewards (% not decided yet) Cloud-hosted staking providers are likely to receive the largest distribution after the locking period due to the degree of decentralization it allows for. The 10 million SAFE tokens available right away to GnosisDAO will likely be used for market making of SAFE tokens for GnosisDAO’s value generation. After all these SAFE tokens were issued to incentivize the success of SAFE on the GnosisChain through joint ownership as well as a collaboration by SafeDAO and GnosisDAO. This proposal’s full implementation may be delayed this year since the transferability of the Safe token is still locked as of now. The token transferability should be enabled by end of November which will help emphasize the importance of this proposal.

Importance:

This proposal has high importance because of the effect on the degree of decentralization Gnosis DAO has. The time it will take for this proposal to realize the full impact is dependent on the time it takes to fully distribute the SAFE token allocations. Realistically as of right now, this proposal is likely not of high importance, but in the long term success of what is proposed will prove to be very important. This proposal is carrying out the intentions of GIP-29 when the SafeDAO spin-off and launch of SAFE token were introduced. GIP-29 intended to decentralize the GNO holder structure to enable the introduction of ecosystem reward programs and value capture through visualizing the future token utility. Furthermore, it aimed at enabling a joint GnosisDAO and SafeDAO treasury to align both communities. Proposal GIP-64 aims to bootstrap this initiative with decentralization through the redistribution of SAFE token allocations to foster autonomous participation between both communities while pursuing a decentralized governance and network. The ability to measure the impact of this proposal may affect it’s realized importance. Whether the sentiment is of low or high importance, the proposal is an operational initiative that supports the integrity of the Gnosis Chain network.
