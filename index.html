Maker Governance Process

Participating in Maker Governance:

MIP0 (Maker Improvement Proposal 0) introduced the Maker improvement proposals (MIPs framework into the Maker ecosystem which provided the foundational steps towards participatory design in MakerDAO’s governance process. This first MIP provides the necessary templates, processes, and guidelines for working with the framework and defines the key roles required for operating the MIPs Process. Creating a robust governance infrastructure through the incentives built within a participatory design. The steps for participation in MakerDAO governance are as follows:

1. Holding MKR Token

The MKR token is the governance token for the Maker Protocol that allows MIPs to be voted on effectively, as well as a recapitalization source when the Maker Protocol is running at a deficit. That means holders of the token can vote on changes to the Maker Protocol and have strong incentives to govern the system well. Anyone can submit proposals for an MKR vote, not just MKR holders. The main responsibility for MKR holders is to use voting power to participate in Governance Polls and Executive Votes.

  • Governance Polls are on-chain proposals that measure the sentiment of the MKR voter’s in the Maker ecosystem
  • Executive Votes are on-chain proposals that execute technical changes to the Maker Protocol through continuous approval voting where the state of the system is continuously represented encourage continuous governance

    (Every Executive Vote is preceded by a Governance Vote)

2. Creating On-Chain Proposals

The MakerDAO governance process has three specific proposal formats for members to participate in governance: Signal Requests, Declarations of Intent, and Maker Improvement Proposals (MIPs). Proposals are introduced to the community, discussed, and put up for a Governance Poll and Executive Vote as necessary.

Proposals may be divided into two categories:

  • Proactive proposals suggest changes to the system and provide a timeframe for executing those changes.
  • Reactive proposals are made in response to changes in the state of the system.

2.1 Signal Requests

These are short proposals used to make changes to the existing parameters in the MakerDAO smart contract system. Successful Signal Requests are aggregated and voted on in a weekly executive votes.

2.2 Declarations of Intent

MIP13 introduced declarations of intent, which allow Maker Governance to signal an intention to the wider community. A declaration of intent is a statement that declares what Maker Governance plans to do. This is so that people will know what Maker Governance is up to and can help them plan their own actions. In many cases, a declaration of intent can be off-chain as a signal request but depending on the specificity and brevity of the declaration it can be on-chain as multiple MIPs.

2.3 Maker Improvement Proposal (MIP)

MIPs are used to define permanent inclusions into MakerDAO smart contracts or the structure of MakerDAO. MIPs have the longest lead time and are proposed on-chain as part of a monthly cycle following a feedback period on the forum

There are two proposal types:

  • General Proposals should be used for proposals whenever a more specific ratified template is not more appropriate
  • Technical Proposals should be used when proposing changes to the smart contract code within the Maker Protocol

Proposal Ideas:

MIPs usually start from an author who has an idea for a MIP and creates a proposal on the MakerDAO forum in the governance proposal ideas category. This is where a proposal can be reviewed and edited to eventually create a PR (pull request) on the Maker MIP Github repo. The proposal must generally follow these templates and it's a good idea to first discuss the proposal idea on the governance forum to get feedback and see if there is enough support. If there is, you can have a formal vote on the governance portal.

To ensure further clarity and completeness, proposals are classified through general and process components. Majority of the time, process MIPs introduce sub proposals for different components which give context to general MIPs components. After following the templates to post in the Maker forum and then creating a pull request in MIPs Github repository the proposal will be likely be approved by a MIP editor. If the editor’s verification of a proposal is successful then it receives is form MIP number based on it’s components and is merged with the pull request to be accepted as a Request for Comments (which is just adding a new MIP into the main codebase).

The Requests for Comments (RFC):

The Request for Comments, is a period of time in which the community is invited to review a draft and offer feedback after a MIP editor verifies the proposal. This period lasts for at least three months, and is followed by a "frozen period" of at least one month during which no further changes are made. Following this time period, the submitted proposal will be ready for Formal Submission.

Formal Submissions (FS):

After this phase a proposal is ready for formal submission according to the governance cycle, usually during the first 3 days of the first week of every month. Whether you’re a creating an opportunity for a governance poll where you propose to vote in a new team or for a executive vote to introduce a new collateral type, the process is robust enough giving the opportunity to anyone to enact meaningful change to the system.

3. Voting Participation On-Chain

Voting is conducted primarily on the governance portal. To use must lock up your MKR in the voting contract because only locked MKR token. When voting with MKR you need to use your wallet to decide whether to vote by locking your maker in a executive voting contract or instead voting in the governance polling contract.

3.1 Governance Polls

Governance Polls are on-chain and occur to measure the sentiment of MKR voters. They use a ranked-choice voting mechanism where voters can select multiples options with the ranked-choices being prioritized for the next poll. Specifically these polls are used for:

  • Determining the DAO and governance processes in the applied non-technical layer of Maker’s Protocol
  • Forming consensus on community values through specific strategic goals and the respective targets
  • Measuring people's opinions on possible Executive Vote proposals
  • Agreeing on governance proposals that come from the MakerDAO forum signal threads
  • Deciding what values certain system parameters should be set to before those values are confirmed in an executive vote
  • Agreeing on risk parameters for new collateral types as presented by Risk Teams

3.2 Executive Voting

Executive votes are on-chain and occur to execute technical changes to the Maker Protocol. Using a continuous approval voting, when an executive vote is active it has proposed changes that are being made on the protocol’s smart-contracts in parallel. Executive votes are likely to be used for:

  • Adding or removing collateral types
  • Adding or removing vault types
  • Adjusting global system and vault specific parameters
  • Replacing modular smart-contracts

Gnosis Improvement Proposal #64

Snapshot Proposal:

Summary:

As decided in GIP-29, GnosisDAO will receive 15% of the total SAFE token supply over 4 years. They suggest using the SAFE tokens to support the main objective of GnosisDAO, which is to make the Gnosis Chain successful. GnosisDAO becomes more successful by distributing SAFE tokens because it decentralizes Gnosis Chain network as a whole. A 94.22% majority of voting members are for the distribution of SAFE tokens to incentivize decentralizing Gnosis Chain through GnosisDAO. The value proposition of this proposal is that it is a foundational step in positioning Gnosis Chain as the most decentralized network available. To support this objective, they suggest distributing the SAFE tokens as additional yield to those participating in staking on the Gnosis Beacon Chain. The SAFE tokens should be distributed equally among GNO holders on a monthly basis until the end of the locking period (February 15, 2023). After the end of the locking period, up to 9.1% of the remaining SAFE tokens should be used as additional yield for stakers on the Gnosis Beacon Chain over the remaining vesting period. The distribution should be based on the importance of the different staking categories, such as individual stakers, distributed liquid staking, professional staking services, and liquid staking.

What the distribution will look like:

Until February 15th all GNO staked will be treated equally in terms of the distribution. After then the different types of stakers will recieve different percentages of the SAFE token rewards (% not decided yet) Cloud-hosted staking providers are likely to recieve the largest distribution after the locking period due to the degree of decentralization it allows for. The 10 million SAFE tokens available right away to GnosisDAO will likely be use for market making of SAFE tokens for GnosisDAO’s value generation. After all these SAFE tokens were issued to incentivize the success of SAFE on the GnosisChain through the joint ownership as well as collaboration by SafeDAO and GnosisDAO. This proposal’s full implementation may be delayed this year since the transferability of the Safe token is still locked as of now. The token transferability should be enabled by end of November which will help emphasize the importance of this proposal.

Importance:

This proposal has a high importance because of the effect on the degree of decentralization Gnosis DAO has. The time it will take for this proposal to realize the full impact is dependent on the time it takes to fully distribute the SAFE token allocations. This proposal is carrying out the intentions of GIP-29, when SafeDAO spin-off and launch of SAFE token were introduce. The intentions of GIP-29 were ultimately to decentralize the GNO holder structure to enable the introduction of ecosystem reward programs and value capture through visualizing the future token utility. Furthermore, it aimed at enabling a joint GnosisDAO and SafeDAO treasury to align both communities. Proposal GIP-64 aims to bootstrap this initiative with the decentralization through the distribution of SAFE token allocations to foster autonomous participation between both communities while pursuing a decentralized governance and network.
